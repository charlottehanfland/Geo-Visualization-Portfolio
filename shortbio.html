Charlotte Hanfland Geographic Analyst

The image is of a famous tumblr thread that I think was instrumental in my early exploration of mapping agriculture and the environment :)

I have always been fascinated by maps and the many, many visualization options when it comes to presenting spatial data. It makes sense that I eventually chose a data-centric focus for my major and it's unsurprising that maps have become my preferred method of visualization. I have most enjoyed times during my studies when I can examine the effects of agriculture on the environment and humans. My most recent project involves mapping CAFO locations against cancer incidence and kidney and respiratory disease deaths. I have also used remote sensing to analyze vegetation health, and I am intersted in using GIS to develop suitability models for conservation practices.

I plan to work one day as a geospatial analyst speacializing in remote sensing. Ideally, my work would involve measuring, monitoring, reporting, and verification of different policy initiatives related to the environment and pollution mitigation. Geovisualization will help me in the future when communicating geospatial data to clients and policymakers.
